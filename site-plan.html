Chocolate chip cookies:
The best chocolate chip cookies I have ever have. Fluffy and good tasting cookies.
Butter, sugar, light brown sugar, vanilla, eggs, flour, baking soda, salt, chocolate chips.
2 room temp sticks of butter
1 cup of white Sugar
½ of packed Light brown Sugar
2 teaspoons of vanilla
2 eggs
3 cups of flour
1 teaspoon of baking soda
½ teaspoon salt
1 ½ cup of chocolate chips

Preheat oven to 350 degrees.
Put it in the microwave to melt it but make sure it is not too hot.
Put the butter in a large mixing bowl and add the brown and white sugar
Beat the butter and the sugars with a hand mixer or standing mixer
Add the vanilla and eggs mix just until combined
Add the flour, baking soda, and salt. Mix until crumbles then mix press the dough together.
Then add the chocolate chips and mix more with your hands
Roll into ball shape and put on a baking sheet
Put in the oven for around 13 minutes.

Mini Meatloaves
A twist on the traditional meatloaf. A cross between meatloaf and meatballs.
Ground beef, onion, ritz crackers, eggs, milk, salt, and pepper.
2 lbs. ground beef
1 onion, diced
1 sleeve of Ritz Crackers
2 eggs
1/4 cup milk, if it seems dry
Salt and pepper

Preheat oven to 350 degrees.
Spray cooking spray into muffin tin. Smash crackers with your fingers and place in a large bowl.
Add other ingredients, except for meat and milk. Use fork to mix together.
Add meat and work through with your fingers. You may add milk if it seems too dry. (I never have)
Form meatloaf mixture into balls about the right size for the muffin tin.
Cook muffin tin over a cookie sheet to catch grease that overflows. Cook for about 30 minutes or until it reaches 165.
Use thermometer to ensure the center of the center meatloaf is 165 degrees.
To add more flavor you could add ketchup
